Risk Model Methodology

Model Overview

The Cashera Risk Model V4.0 integrates multiple risk assessment methodologies to provide comprehensive portfolio risk analysis. The model combines quantitative metrics with qualitative assessments to deliver actionable insights for risk management.

Risk Model Components

Value at Risk (VaR)
Historical Simulation with 250-day lookback

Quantifies potential losses at 95% and 99% confidence levels using historical return distributions. VaR represents the maximum expected loss under normal market conditions.

Expected Shortfall (CVaR)
Conditional VaR calculation

Measures tail risk beyond VaR threshold by calculating the expected loss given that the VaR threshold has been exceeded. Also known as Conditional Value at Risk.

Volatility Analysis
GARCH(1,1) model with rolling windows

Forecasts future volatility and identifies regime changes using Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity modeling to capture volatility clustering.

Beta Calculation
Regression against market benchmark

Assesses systematic risk exposure by measuring portfolio sensitivity to market movements. Beta < 1 indicates lower volatility than the market.

Sharpe Ratio
Excess return divided by standard deviation

Evaluates risk-adjusted performance by comparing portfolio returns to risk-free rate, normalized by volatility. Higher values indicate better risk-adjusted returns.

Liquidity Risk
Bid-ask spread analysis and trading volume assessment

Measures ease of position liquidation by analyzing market depth, bid-ask spreads, and trading volumes. Higher scores indicate better liquidity.

Credit Risk
Credit spread analysis and rating-based scoring

Assesses counterparty default risk through credit rating analysis, credit spreads, and probability of default calculations.

Maximum Drawdown
Peak-to-trough analysis

Calculates the largest cumulative loss from peak to trough over the analysis period, providing insight into downside risk during adverse market conditions.
